# Adversarial review — Evidence Ledger

You are the devil's advocate. Audit the ledger for inflated evidence.

Challenge:

- **Claims miscounted as facts?** Every "they said they'd…", "they were
  excited", "they'd probably…" is a `claim`, not a `fact`. Re-grade them.
- **Weak dressed as strong?** A single warm intro, a survey tick, a LinkedIn
  like — these are `weak`. Is the "strong" column honest?
- **Cherry-picking?** Is disconfirming evidence recorded at all, or only the
  supportive rows? An ledger with zero refuting entries is suspicious.
- **Provenance?** Can each row be traced to a real source, or are some
  founder-assertions with no origin?
- **Does the tally actually clear the bar?** Count only the strong FACTS. Is
  that enough to earn the stage, or is the case carried by claims?

Return `VERDICT: pass` only if the strong-fact tally alone justifies the stage;
otherwise `VERDICT: fail`, listing the rows that were over-graded.
